Go-to-Market Strategy for COG: AI-Powered Platform for Job Seekers

Strategic capstone focused on differentiating an early-stage AI startup in a saturated productivity-tool landscape while maintaining ethical positioning.

TimeframeSep - Dec 2025
RoleStrategic Lead & Primary Presenter
Team6-person capstone team
Go-to-Market Competitive Intelligence Brand Positioning Ethical AI

Overview

COG is an early-stage AI startup building a platform that connects job seekers with AI tools. We were asked to design a launch-ready go-to-market strategy that could cut through noise in a crowded category.

Market context: 4.3% unemployment, 14M+ active job seekers, and strong competitor funding momentum. The core question was how to differentiate against better-funded players while preserving a values-driven approach to AI.

The Challenge

  • Build a credible GTM strategy for a startup entering a saturated AI productivity space.
  • Differentiate against well-funded competitors (including $30M+ combined raised by key players).
  • Define positioning that emphasizes AI empowerment over replacement.

My Role

  • Led competitive research, brand positioning, and go-to-market strategy.
  • Owned synthesis and strategic narrative across the semester.
  • Served as primary presenter for founder and faculty delivery.

Tools Used

  • ChatGPT for accelerated competitive intelligence and iterative research.
  • AI-assisted SWOT analysis and market segmentation.
  • Google Workspace and presentation tools for collaboration and output packaging.

Process / Approach

  1. Competitive intelligence: analyzed Cluely ($20M) and Highlight AI ($10M) for features, positioning, and public reception.
  2. Market analysis: identified a gap for an ethical, human-centered approach with an execution mindset.
  3. Positioning: developed “Do Tank not Think Tank” and “Level up humans” framing.
  4. GTM development: built a 4-platform channel strategy plus experiential Do Tank Lab events and partnership roadmap.

Outcomes / Learnings

Delivery & Impact:

  • Presented comprehensive strategy to COG founder and faculty advisor.
  • Recommendations are being used in ongoing discussions.
  • Received positive feedback on positioning and execution plan.

Key Deliverables:

  • 40+ slide strategy deck covering landscape, SWOT, manifesto, media strategy, and campaign concepts.
  • Messaging framework with two positioning options and associated taglines.
  • Partnership roadmap with 20+ potential collaborators across education, corporate, and public sectors.

What I Learned:

  • AI research can compress weeks into days when paired with critical evaluation and synthesis.
  • Gen Z job seekers worked as a focused wedge market to balance ambition with execution.
  • Weekly sprint reviews made stakeholder alignment and iteration significantly stronger.
  • Ethical positioning can be a strategic differentiator, not just a values statement.

What I'd Do Differently:

  • Run user research with job seekers earlier in the process.
  • Tighten product definition sooner where interface/features were still ambiguous.
  • Add more quantitative market sizing for partnership prioritization.
