Go-to-Market Strategy for COG: AI-Powered Platform for Job Seekers

Strategic capstone focused on differentiating an early-stage AI startup in a saturated productivity-tool landscape while maintaining ethical positioning.

TimeframeSep - Dec 2025
RoleStrategic Lead & Primary Presenter
Team6-person capstone team
Go-to-Market Competitive Intelligence Brand Positioning Ethical AI

Problem Framing

The Discovery

COG is an early-stage AI startup building a platform that connects job seekers with AI tools. The team needed a go-to-market strategy that could launch credibly, quickly, and with a clear point of view.

The Market Gap

We assessed a market with 14M+ active job seekers and major noise from AI productivity tools. Competitors were either productivity-heavy or controversy-driven, which exposed whitespace for a more ethical, human-centered position.

The Vision

Position COG as execution-first AI support: “Do Tank not Think Tank,” with the broader framing “Level up humans.” The intent was AI as empowerment rather than replacement.

Constraints

  • Saturated AI tool landscape and strong funding momentum from competitors.
  • Early-stage product ambiguity around interface/features during strategy work.
  • Semester-bound timeline requiring rapid synthesis and stakeholder alignment.

Success Criteria

  • Deliver a founder-ready GTM strategy with clear differentiation.
  • Define actionable channel, messaging, and partnership priorities.
  • Validate clarity through positive founder/faculty feedback and continued strategy adoption.

Product & Design Thinking

User Segments

  • Primary: students, recent graduates, and early professionals navigating an AI-saturated job market.
  • Secondary: organizations seeking ethical AI collaboration (education, corporate, and public sector).

Research & Insights

  • Used AI-assisted competitive intelligence to compare Cluely ($20M) and Highlight AI ($10M).
  • Applied SWOT and market segmentation to clarify where COG could win.
  • Key insight: values-driven positioning can become strategic leverage when paired with strong execution plans.

Execution (Design to Build)

Design

I translated research into concrete strategy artifacts: messaging options, campaign concept (“Tassels Into Titles”), and channel architecture across TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, and YouTube.

Engineering

This capstone emphasized strategy infrastructure rather than shipping product code. Execution was run in sprint cadence with weekly reviews, structured research workflows, and iterative synthesis to keep recommendations implementation-ready.

Impact

User / Product Outcomes

  • Sharper user wedge and messaging clarity for early-career job seekers.
  • Clearer narrative for how COG supports users without overpromising automation.
  • Campaign and channel plans that translated strategy into usable execution steps.

Business / Strategic Value

  • Presented a comprehensive strategy package to founder and faculty advisor.
  • Recommendations moved into ongoing decision discussions, indicating stakeholder adoption.
  • Created reusable strategic assets: positioning framework, partnership roadmap, and GTM narrative.

Learnings & Tradeoffs

Key Tradeoffs Made

  • Prioritized Gen Z job seekers as a focused wedge instead of broad market coverage.
  • Prioritized strategic clarity over deep product-interface exploration due to scope and ambiguity.
  • Chose ethical differentiation over provocative positioning despite higher short-term visibility pressure.

What I’d Do Next

  • Run user research with job seekers earlier to stress-test positioning assumptions.
  • Define product surface area sooner to strengthen strategy-to-product handoff.
  • Add deeper quantitative sizing to rank partnership targets with more rigor.
