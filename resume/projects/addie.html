addie

A mobile concept for panic and anxiety moments: tap one button and get guided support that transitions from high-alert red toward calm blue.

TimeframeCurrent
RoleProduct Concept + UX Flow Design
PlatformMobile-first app concept
Panic Response Breathing Guidance Grounding Flow Low-friction UX

Overview

The core idea is simple: if someone is having an anxiety or panic attack, their phone is often the closest tool. addie is designed to reduce friction in that moment and guide users step by step toward regulation.

Problem / Why It Matters

During panic, even small decisions feel difficult. Many wellness flows assume users can think clearly and navigate menus, which is unrealistic in high-stress moments. The experience must work immediately, not after setup-heavy interactions.

My Role

  • Defined the concept, scenario, and low-friction interaction priorities.
  • Mapped transition states: panic entry -> breathing -> check-in -> grounding.
  • Translated behavior goals into concrete screen concepts and visual pacing.

Process / What I Did

  1. Designed onboarding prompt for a safe nickname so the app can address users in a comforting way.
  2. Created a default first screen with a large red “Help me” button and minimal text at the bottom.
  3. Defined breathing flow with expanding/contracting ball and Inhale/Exhale timing cue.
  4. Added checkpoint: “Feeling calmer?” to either progress or continue support.
  5. Built grounding flow using 5-senses prompts tailored for anxiety/panic contexts.
  6. Used a color transition from bright red toward blue to reflect nervous-system de-escalation.

Outcomes / Learnings

  • Low-friction flows are critical when cognitive load is high.
  • Color and pacing can reinforce emotional safety, not just visual style.
  • Branching check-ins (“calmer?”) keep support adaptive without overwhelming users.
